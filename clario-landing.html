Your decision
"I have been offered a senior director role at a tech company in London. The salary is double what I earn now in Amsterdam, but I would need to relocate and leave behind a life I have built over six years. I have a partner and we are not married yet. Should I take it?"
R
Rex · Fire · The Emperor
Double salary is a category change, not an increment. The London tech market opens doors Amsterdam simply cannot. The real question is not whether to go — it is whether you have a 12-month plan to either bring your partner or return having built real leverage. What does your number look like to make this work?
N
Nadia · Water · The Oracle
Rex, you skipped the most important variable. An unmarried partner and a six-year life together. Before this becomes a financial calculation it needs to be an honest conversation. Have you actually asked your partner what they want — not what they would accept?
M
Mira · Earth · The Architect
Amsterdam to London is a 40-minute flight under £100. At double salary you can visit every two weeks and still save significantly more than you do now. Run the actual numbers before framing this as abandoning your relationship. This is a configuration change, not a departure.
D
Dante · Void · The Shadow
Nobody has asked the question that changes everything: why are you framing this as a solo decision? If your partner cannot or will not come, that tells you something important about the relationship — not just the job. I would want to know which problem you are actually solving here.
